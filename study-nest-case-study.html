🚨 Problems

Through my research and personal experience, I discovered that many students struggle with finding effective study environments and managing their learning schedules.

📚

Students lack access to quiet study spaces, which prevents them from focusing effectively on their coursework.

⏰

Many students struggle with time management and study planning, leading to poor academic performance.

📱

The absence of a centralized platform that combines study planning, environment finding, and progress tracking can lead to increased stress levels.

💡 Solution

Students need a comprehensive platform that helps them find optimal study environments, manage their study schedules, and track their learning progress in one convenient location.

📱 Study Nest

A mobile app that:

🏠

Helps students find and book quiet study spaces

📅

Provides personalized study planning and scheduling tools

📊

Offers progress tracking and motivation features

Ideate

Card Sort

With the help of UXtweaks, I was able to send out card sorts to 8 participants. I learned that most participants grouped study planning and progress tracking into the same category as productivity tools. Participants placed study space booking and social study groups in separate categories. These results led me to create distinct sections for different types of study activities.

Reflections

🎯 User-Centered Design

I've learned that UX design isn't about what I think is best, it's entirely about how students interact with the product - my first set of prototypes I had thought was easy to understand, since I had created it. When I saw how students used it, I realized how unclear or unnecessary certain features were.

🧪 Usability Testing Impact

If it's not usable for them, then it's not useful - In my first high-fidelity design, I thought everything was perfect. However, during usability testing, I quickly realized that the navigation was confusing and that I was missing key features students needed.

🔄 Feedback & Iteration

Feedback is a gift - My design was able to grow much more when it came to receiving thoughts and opinions from others, who could see things about the product that I had not seen.
