Reflections

ğŸ¯ User-Centered Design

I've learned that UX design isn't about what I think is best, it's entirely about how students interact with the product - my first set of prototypes I had thought was easy to understand, since I had created it. When I saw how students used it, I realized how unclear or unnecessary certain features were.

ğŸ§ª Usability Testing Impact

If it's not usable for them, then it's not useful - In my first high-fidelity design, I thought everything was perfect. However, during usability testing, I quickly realized that the navigation was confusing and that I was missing key features students needed.

ğŸ”„ Feedback & Iteration

Feedback is a gift - My design was able to grow much more when it came to receiving thoughts and opinions from others, who could see things about the product that I had not seen.
