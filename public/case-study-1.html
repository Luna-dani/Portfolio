Project Overview

Olive Health & Wellness, a growing health and wellness platform, needed a complete redesign to better serve their diverse user base. The existing platform had accessibility issues, poor mobile experience, and a confusing navigation structure that was preventing users from finding the health resources they needed.

My role was to lead a user-centered redesign that would improve accessibility, enhance the mobile experience, and create an intuitive interface that would help users easily access health information and wellness resources.

The Challenge

  • Accessibility barriers preventing users with disabilities from accessing health information
  • Poor mobile experience making it difficult to access resources on-the-go
  • Complex navigation structure causing users to get lost when searching for specific health topics
  • Outdated design that didn't reflect the calming, trustworthy nature of health and wellness
  • Lack of clear information hierarchy for different types of health content

My Process

1. Research & Discovery

I began with comprehensive user research, including user interviews, analytics review, and competitive analysis. This helped me understand user pain points and identify opportunities for improvement.

User Research Process

2. Information Architecture

I restructured the site's navigation and content hierarchy to create a more intuitive user flow. This included simplifying the main navigation and creating clear pathways to key products and categories.

Information Architecture

3. Wireframing & Prototyping

I created detailed wireframes and interactive prototypes to test different design approaches. This allowed me to iterate quickly and validate design decisions before moving to visual design.

Wireframes & Prototypes

4. Visual Design

I developed a modern, clean visual design that prioritized readability and usability while maintaining the brand's identity. The design system included consistent spacing, typography, and color usage.

Visual Design

5. User Testing & Iteration

I conducted multiple rounds of user testing to validate the design and identify areas for improvement. This iterative process ensured the final design met user needs and business goals.

User Testing

The Solution

I delivered a completely redesigned health and wellness platform that addressed all the identified challenges:

Accessibility-First Design

WCAG 2.1 AA compliant design with proper contrast ratios, screen reader support, and keyboard navigation

Mobile-First Approach

Responsive design optimized for mobile devices with touch-friendly interactions and easy access to health resources

Intuitive Information Architecture

Clear content hierarchy and simplified navigation that helps users quickly find the health information they need

Calming Visual Design

Soothing color palette and typography that reflects the trustworthy, calming nature of health and wellness

User-Centered Content

Content organized by user needs and health topics rather than internal business structure

Results & Impact

+78% Accessibility Score
+65% Mobile Usability
-45% Time to Find Info
+89% User Satisfaction

Key Learnings

This project reinforced several important principles:

  • Accessibility should be built into the design from the start, not added as an afterthought
  • Health and wellness platforms require special consideration for user trust and emotional state
  • Information architecture is critical when dealing with complex health topics
  • Mobile-first design is especially important for health resources that users access on-the-go
  • User research with diverse populations is essential for creating truly inclusive designs
